Algebraic Concepts Explained

  • Linear Equations: The simplest form of equation where the highest power of the variable is 1. Solved by isolating the variable.
  • Quadratic Equations: Equations of the second degree. Solved using the quadratic formula: x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / 2a.
  • Systems of Equations: A set of equations with multiple variables. Solved by substitution or elimination.
  • Matrices: Rectangular arrays of numbers. The determinant provides crucial information about the matrix, such as whether it's invertible.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I solve for x?

To solve for x, you must isolate it on one side of the equation by performing the same operations on both sides. Our calculator automates this for various equation types.

What is a system of equations?

A system of equations is a collection of two or more equations with a same set of unknowns. The solution is the point where all equations are satisfied simultaneously.

What does the determinant of a matrix mean?

The determinant is a scalar value that can be computed from the elements of a square matrix. If the determinant is zero, the matrix does not have an inverse.
