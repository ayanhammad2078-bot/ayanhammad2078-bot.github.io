How to Use the GPA Calculator

  1. Enter the name of your course (optional, for reference).
  2. Select the letter grade you received for that course.
  3. Enter the number of credit hours for that course.
  4. Click + Add Course to add more courses.
  5. Click Calculate GPA to see your weighted GPA.

Understanding Your GPA Result

GPA is calculated as the sum of (grade points × credit hours) divided by total credit hours. A 4.0 GPA is the highest possible on a standard 4.0 scale. Most universities require a minimum GPA of 2.0 to remain in good academic standing, and many graduate programs require 3.0 or higher.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is GPA?

GPA stands for Grade Point Average. It is a standardized way of measuring academic achievement in the United States and many other countries. It is calculated on a 4.0 scale, where an A equals 4.0 grade points.

What is the difference between semester GPA and cumulative GPA?

Semester GPA is calculated using only the courses taken in a single semester. Cumulative GPA is the overall average across all semesters completed. This calculator computes the GPA for the courses you enter, which can represent either.

How do credit hours affect GPA?

Courses with more credit hours have a greater impact on your GPA. For example, a 4-credit course weighs more than a 1-credit course. This is why the GPA formula uses a weighted average based on credit hours.

What GPA do I need for graduate school?

Most graduate programs require a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.0 (B average). Highly competitive programs may require 3.5 or higher. Some programs also consider GPA trends, test scores, and other factors.
