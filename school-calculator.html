How to Use the School Calculator

  • Basic Operations: Click number buttons and operators (+, −, ×, ÷) to build your expression.
  • Trigonometric Functions: Use sin, cos, tan for angle calculations. Input angles in degrees.
  • Powers & Roots: Use x^y for exponents and √ for square roots.
  • Logarithms: Use log for base-10 logarithms and ln for natural logarithms.
  • Factorials: Click n! to calculate the factorial of a number.
  • Absolute Value: Use |x| to get the absolute value of a number.
  • Clear & Delete: AC clears everything, DEL removes the last character.
  • Calculate: Press = to see your result instantly.

Understanding Your Results

The School Edition Calculator displays both your input expression and the calculated result. The green screen mimics the authentic LCD display of the TI-84 Plus, making it familiar to students who use these calculators in their math classes. All calculations are performed in real-time, and you can see your expression as you type it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this calculator accurate for school exams?

Yes! This calculator uses the same mathematical functions and logic as the TI-84 Plus, making it a perfect study tool and practice calculator for geometry and algebra exams.

How do I calculate sine, cosine, or tangent?

Click the sin, cos, or tan button, then enter your angle in degrees. For example, to find sin(30°), click sin, type 30, and press =. The result will be 0.5.

Can I use this calculator during school exams?

This online calculator is great for homework, studying, and practice. Check with your school's exam policies to see if you can use a physical TI-84 or similar calculator during tests.

How do I calculate a power or exponent?

Click the x^y button, enter your base number, click x^y again, enter your exponent, and press =. For example: 2 x^y 3 = gives you 8 (2 to the power of 3).

What does the 2nd and ALPHA button do?

These buttons are included for authenticity. In a real TI-84, they unlock secondary functions. In this version, they're visual elements that match the real calculator's design.
