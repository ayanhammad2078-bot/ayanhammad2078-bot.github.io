Understanding Trigonometric Functions

Sine (sin): The ratio of the opposite side to the hypotenuse in a right triangle.
Cosine (cos): The ratio of the adjacent side to the hypotenuse.
Tangent (tan): The ratio of the opposite side to the adjacent side (sin/cos).
Inverse functions: Find the angle when you know the ratio value.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between degrees and radians?

Degrees and radians are two ways to measure angles. A full circle is 360 degrees or 2π radians. Radians are the standard unit in mathematics and physics. To convert: radians = degrees × π/180.

What is the sine of 90 degrees?

The sine of 90 degrees (or π/2 radians) is exactly 1. This represents the maximum value sine can reach.

What are inverse trigonometric functions used for?

Inverse trig functions (arcsin, arccos, arctan) find the angle when you know the ratio. For example, if sin(θ) = 0.5, then arcsin(0.5) = 30 degrees.

Why is tangent undefined at 90 degrees?

Tangent = sin/cos. At 90 degrees, cosine equals 0, which makes the division undefined. This is why tan(90°) does not have a real value.
