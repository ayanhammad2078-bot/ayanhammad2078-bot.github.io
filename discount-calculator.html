How to Use the Discount Calculator

  • Mode 1: Enter the original price and discount percentage to find the final sale price and savings amount.
  • Mode 2: Enter the original price and the sale price to find out what percentage discount was applied.
  • Mode 3: Enter an original price and two stacked discounts to see the final price after both are applied sequentially.

Understanding Discount Results

A discount percentage reduces the original price by that fraction. Two stacked discounts are not simply added together — a 20% discount followed by a 10% discount is not the same as a 30% discount. The second discount is applied to the already-reduced price, resulting in a total effective discount of 28% in this example.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate a discount?

To find the sale price: Sale Price = Original Price × (1 - Discount% / 100). To find the savings: Savings = Original Price × (Discount% / 100). For example, a 25% discount on $120: Sale Price = $120 × 0.75 = $90; Savings = $30.

Are stacked discounts the same as adding them together?

No. Stacked discounts are applied sequentially, not additively. A 20% discount followed by a 10% discount gives an effective total discount of 28%, not 30%. This is because the second discount applies to the already-reduced price.

How do I find the original price from a sale price?

If you know the sale price and the discount percentage, the original price = Sale Price / (1 - Discount% / 100). For example, if an item is $90 after a 25% discount, the original price = $90 / 0.75 = $120.

What is the difference between a discount and a markdown?

In retail, a discount is a temporary price reduction offered to customers, while a markdown is a permanent reduction in the selling price of an item. Both are calculated the same way mathematically.
